Finally I got a new Japanese Mechanical Keyboard

17 May 2021 | 2 min read

I’m really tired of the freak gaming hardwares and I bought an Epson Q203A. She has Fujitsu Peerless switches and this is a bit diffrent from modern switches. Actuation force is really more than MX or other Chinese switches but it is a characteristic point for her. Main frame and keycaps look so clean as you can see and does not have yellowish stains. Let’s get to the main issue, she has five-pin male 180° DIN connector (yes she has a male plug pls respect her :D) and it isn’t compatible to modern systems. I’ve seen several DIN to USB marker project on marker markets and other geek stores but I still live in Turkey and have some financial problems against USD :’) If I’ve bought a connector from these markets, I’d have paid about 30-45 dollar(included shipping). It is really substantial amount of money. So I’ve ordered a five-pin female DIN to PS/2 converter on Aliexpress and I’ll buy a PS/2 to USB converter from a local tech store. Actually, I can convert it by soldering but I want to keep its originality. When they arrived, I’ll post an update for converting stuff. Peace out.

‏

03.06.2021

I’ve got five-pin female 180° DIN to six-pin male PS/2 converter and female PS/2 to USB converter but I couldn’t get it run. When I’ve plugged all cables and connectors, USB connector warmed up extremly. I have tried on my desktop, laptop and brother’s laptop but keyboard hasn’t responded at all. I guess first connector is broken. I’ll try another DIN5 connector and if that doesn’t work either, going to buy a RasPi Zero and solder my AT/T socket to its GPIO Header but I don’t know how I’ll do, do not even know mikroC.

06.06.2021

I’ve ripped off the keyboard and seen there are a membran circuit and old-fashioned PCB. Actually I want to replace the whole PCB but I have no general circuit & keyboard matrices knowledges for that. I don’t even know which part of daughter board will be soldered to Arduino or whatever. Some people had recommend mapping the matrix on the current PCB and wire that to a Teensy / Arduino. And completed some researches. I’ve asked how to do on Redd*t and they said that’s an XT keyboard and got suggestion about DIY converter with a Teesy board but those boards ridiculously overpriced in Turkey so I bought a new adapter from locals. If it doesn’t work, I’m gonna try the DIY one and if none of them work, I’ll map the matrix and wire to Arduino.
